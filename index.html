SUCCESS STRATEGIES FOR STRUCTURE OF EXPRESSIONS

For Students

  • Show ALL work - even if you get the wrong answer, you can earn partial credit
  • Use proper mathematical vocabulary - terms like "factor," "GCF," "difference of squares"
  • Check your work - substitute back into original expressions when possible
  • Organize clearly - use neat, readable mathematical notation
  • Circle final answers - make them easy for graders to identify
  • Use the TI-84 strategically - verify your algebraic work when time permits

Common Student Errors to Avoid

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch Out For These Common Mistakes:
  • Forgetting to factor out GCF before applying other factoring techniques
  • Sign errors when distributing negative numbers
  • Incorrect application of difference of squares pattern
  • Confusing perfect square trinomials with other factoring patterns
  • Not checking factored forms by expanding back to verify

Time Management Tips

  • Spend approximately 2-3 minutes per Part I question
  • Allow 5-8 minutes per Part II question
  • Save time for checking work if possible
  • Don't spend too much time on any single question

Verification Strategies

  1. Substitution Check: Substitute the same value into both original and simplified expressions
  2. Expand and Compare: Expand factored forms to verify they match the original
  3. Coefficient Matching: Ensure like terms have correct coefficients after combining
  4. Distributive Property Check: Verify all terms were distributed correctly
  5. Sign Verification: Double-check that all positive and negative signs are correct
  6. GCF Validation: Multiply GCF back through to confirm original expression

Pattern Recognition Skills

  • Variable Patterns: x, x¬≤, x¬≥ progression; xy, x¬≤y, xy¬≤ patterns
  • Fraction Patterns: 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 2/3, 3/4 (common denominators)
  • Negative Sign Patterns: Recognize -(a + b) = -a - b distribution

Problem-Solving Approach

  1. Identify: Determine what type of simplification is needed (combine, factor, expand)

  3. Execute: Work step-by-step, writing each transformation clearly

  5. Verify: Check answer using substitution or reverse operation

Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (2 credits each)

  • 2 Credits: Correct answer selected
  • 0 Credits: Incorrect answer or no response

Note: No partial credit is awarded for multiple choice questions in Part I

Part II: Constructed Response Questions (2-4 credits each)

4 Credits - Excellent Response

  • Complete and correct mathematical solution
  • Clear, logical presentation of work with all steps shown
  • Proper mathematical notation and vocabulary throughout
  • All algebraic manipulations justified and accurate
  • Answer clearly indicated and properly labeled

3 Credits - Good Response

  • Substantially correct solution with minor computational errors
  • Generally clear work shown with most essential steps present
  • Minor gaps in reasoning that don't affect overall mathematical approach
  • Mostly correct mathematical notation with few errors
  • Answer reasonably close to correct solution

2 Credits - Satisfactory Response

  • Partially correct solution demonstrating understanding of key concepts
  • Some correct mathematical procedures and methods shown
  • May contain significant computational errors or incomplete reasoning
  • Work partially shown but missing some important steps
  • Shows familiarity with required algebraic techniques

1 Credit - Minimal Response

  • Minimal understanding of mathematical concepts demonstrated
  • Some correct procedures or concepts identified but poorly executed
  • Major errors in computation, factoring, or algebraic manipulation
  • Little work shown or presentation unclear
  • Limited grasp of required mathematical skills

0 Credits - No Credit

  • No understanding of mathematical concepts demonstrated
  • Incorrect procedures used throughout or no mathematical work shown
  • Response completely off-topic or no response provided
  • No evidence of required algebraic or factoring skills
